# Blinkit
Collaborative Product Management case study repository focused on user flow, friction analysis, and product improvement insights for real-world applications.

### Purpose

This is a quick commerce delivery portal that promises to deliver any product or commodity within 10 minutes.

---

## Step 1 → Teardown

|Section         |Flow       |Friction   |
|----------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
|Home page|**Customized**, context-relevant updates are displayed for all types of users, supported by a well-structured category-based UI. <br><br> **User’s location and estimated delivery time (ETA)** are shown on the homepage before selecting any product.| **For 1st time or infrequent users**, homepage may feel overwhelming because of to the high volume of displayed information which can at time alter buying decision of customer. <br><br> **Users** are required to grant location permission before accessing the app experience|
|Search bar|**Search bar** displays basic staple food items even before the user starts typing, helping users recall essential household items they might otherwise forget. <br><br> **Product names** are displayed independently of any specific category. **Voice-based** search functionality is also available for user convenience |**Search recommendation** system shows inconsistencies where brand names and category terms overlap, leading to potential ambiguity in search results.|
|Cart page |**Clean and well-organized**, with clear product details, accurate pricing, and relevant recommendations. |**Lack of transparency** in pricing leads to user frustration, as additional charges are not prominently displayed.|
|Payment page| Multiple options available, allowing users to make a suitable selection. | No friction for Users |
