# Blinkit
Collaborative Product Management case study repository focused on user flow, friction analysis, and product improvement insights for real-world applications.

### Purpose

This is a quick commerce delivery portal that promises to deliver any product or commodity within 10 minutes.

---

## Step 1 → Teardown

| Section | Flow | Friction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Home page** | Customized, context-relevant updates are displayed for all types of users, supported by a well-structured category-based UI.

The user’s location and estimated delivery time (ETA) are shown on the homepage before selecting any product. | For 1st time or infrequent users, homepage may feel overwhelming because of to the high volume of displayed information which can at time alter buying decision of customer.

Users are required to grant location permission before accessing the app experience |
| **Search bar** | The search bar displays basic staple food items even before the user starts typing, helping users recall essential household items they might otherwise forget.

Product names are displayed independently of any specific category.

Voice-based search functionality is also available for user convenience | The search recommendation system shows inconsistencies where brand names and category terms overlap, leading to potential ambiguity in search results. |
| **Cart page** | The cart page is clean and well-organized, with clear product details, accurate pricing, and relevant recommendations. | Due to lack of transparency in pricing leads to user frustration, as additional charges are not prominently displayed. |
| **Payment page** | There are multiple options available, allowing users to make a suitable selection. | No friction for Users |
